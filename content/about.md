### The Problem
My name is Brian Mulkern and I got to where I am by a long and unlikely path. Maybe I'll tell you about it sometime. While working on creating a business that could help the environment and also provide economic opportunity for others, I hit a roadblock. The data I needed is widely dispersed and much of it too costly to enable the model I was envisioning, so I decided I would source the data myself. It bothers me that the data I need to do something positive already exists, and I am duplicating work. I preach the merits of open source to anyone who will listen, and I work using open source tools. That unease I felt came from a misalignment with my stated goals and ideals, and the truth became unavoidable. I began to realize that while my business model might help change the world for the better, the data it generates could be used in more ways than I can imagine or implement on my own.

### The Bottom Feeder
The term "bottom feeder" has a negative connotation that matches my personal story more than any sane person would publicly admit, but the truth is many denizens of the deep work to help keep the ocean clean. I have been many things and labeled many things, but "sane" was never one of them. I came to the decision that information capable of providing value to society should not be hoarded for profit, and instead should be made freely available to promote accountability and innovation across domains. I am creating **benthic.io** as a seabed in the ocean of information. It is a home for data that can create a positive public impact, making it not only freely available but easily accessible by modern tools and workflows. The debut dataset is about accountability in government spending, and its creation and maintenance are helping hone my personal skill set so that I can continue to build and improve.

### The Next Step
Maybe you're wondering what this business I spoke of is about. That is still cooking and I can't give away everything until it's ready. I can tell you that it has nothing to do with the government or politics, and everything to do with waste reduction and supply chain resilience. The data that it generates will be valuable (people are selling it right now) and will be hosted here for everyone to use. I am going to give it away because I am delusional enough to think I can help change the world, and that by being open with people about my goals and sharing value freely I might even get some help doing it. Speaking of help, I desperately need some. The last public-facing service I created ran over telnet and dial-up, so feedback and suggestions are very welcome.
