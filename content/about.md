### The Person and The Problem
My name is Brian Mulkern and I got to where I am by a long and unlikely path, maybe I'll tell you about it sometime. While working on creating a business that could help the environment and also provide economic opportunity for others I hit a roadblock. The data I needed is widely dispersed and much of it too costly to enable the model I was envisioning, and so I decided I would source the data myself. It bothered me that the data I needed to do something positive already existed, and I was duplicating work. I preach the merits of open source to anyone that will listen. I build using open source tools. That unease I felt was misalignment with my stated goals and ideals, and the truth became unavoidable. I began to realize that while my business model might help change the world for the better, the data it generates could be used in more ways than I can imagine or implement on my own.

### The Ocean and the Bottom Feeder
The term bottom feeder has a negative connotation that matches my personal story more than any sane person would publically admit. The thing is, I am completely delusional so I made the descision that information capable of providing value to society should not be hoarded for profit, and instead should be made freely available to promote accountability and innovation across domains. I have created **benthic.io** as a seabed in the ocean of information. It is a home for data that can create a positive public impact, making it not only freely available but easily accessible by modern tools and workflows.
